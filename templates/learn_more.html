What is Sign Language Translation?

Sign language translation uses artificial intelligence and computer vision to recognize hand gestures and convert them into text and speech in real-time. Our technology bridges the communication gap between sign language users and the hearing community.

Traditional communication methods often create barriers for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. Our AI-powered translator removes these barriers, enabling seamless, instant communication without the need for human interpreters.

How Our Technology Works

1. Computer Vision

Our system uses advanced computer vision algorithms to track hand movements, positions, and gestures through your device's camera. Every frame is analyzed in real-time to identify sign language patterns.

2. Machine Learning Recognition

Deep learning models trained on thousands of sign language gestures recognize and classify each sign with high accuracy. Our AI has been trained to understand:

  • All 26 letters of the American Sign Language (ASL) alphabet
  • Hand shapes and orientations
  • Movement patterns and speeds
  • Context-aware gesture recognition

3. Real-Time Processing

Detected signs are instantly converted into text, displayed on screen, and can be spoken aloud using text-to-speech technology. The entire process happens in under 100 milliseconds.

4. Sentence Building

Users can build complete sentences by adding detected signs one by one, with options to add spaces, delete mistakes, and save or share their messages.
